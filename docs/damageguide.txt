Pokémon Masters Damage Formula Breakdown
Updated to game version 2.25

This guide aims to teach the users how the Damage is calculated in the Pokémon Masters mobile game. The formula was supposed to be related in some way to the one existing in the mainline games but adapted to the reality of the game, since there are many mechanics that are not included directly, like Natures, Abilities, Items, IVs, EVs, etc. The discovery of the formula has been a main effort of some users in the Reddit’s Pokémon Master community and was determined, mainly, by u/Absynthez, u/theSFaction, and ncs12345#1337. As the time went on, many other mechanics and considerations were discovered by the playerbase, which were made public in different places on the Internet but, thankfully, being tracked by some users, including myself. Many of the latest discoveries involve determining the value of the Power Ups by Skills and Move Multipliers.

The current Damage Formula is determined like this:

0<Move Power × Stat Ratio × Battle Conditions × Rolls>

Each part of the formula will be described in the following parts of the document. The idea is that once you’ve read this document (somehow), you can calculate the possible damage rolls in the following scenarios:
Wallace’s Milotic (Level 125, Move Level 4, 5 Stars, 0 Potential, no Theme Skills and this Grid) with Lucky Skill Critical Strike 2, equipped with a 2★ Lvl. 15 Water Bandanna and Sp. Attack boosted up to 6 times counterattacks with a Critical Hit Mirror Coat after losing over 85% HP against a Psychic-weak trapped target that has 154 Sp. Defense in a Psychic-Terrain field dealing 25,165 damage.
Ethan’s Typhlosion (Level 125, Move Level 3, 5 Stars, 0 Potential, no Theme Skills and this Grid), Sp. Attack boosted up to 4 times and health between 34-50% uses Flamethrower against a target that has 156 Sp. Defense, dealing the following damage rolls: 171, 173, 177, 179, 183, 185, 187, 189, 191.

Part 1. Move Power
The Move Power is an integer value that indicates the final value that will be used in the damage calculation and is obtained by the following formula:
Move Power = 0<0<(Power + Grid) × (1 + ΣSkillPowerUps + Boosts)> × Modifier>

Power
You can see this value in the move details when you press it. There’s a difference between the Power of the moves your units use and the Power that the enemies use against you, and that is the Move Level. While your units can reach a Move Level of 5, the enemies will always be at Move Level 1. Each Move Level increases the Power of your damaging moves by 5% up to 20% at maximum Level, rounding down the result to zero decimals. After this, if the unit has an Increaser, like being 6EX while being a Tech role unit or by having the skill Hit the Gas, the move gains a boost of Power. The value displayed in the description of the unit’s move is always the current Power according to the Move Level and Increments. The formula to determine the Power at any given Move Level would be like this:
Power(Base,Level) = 0<0<Base × (100 + (Level - 1) × 5) / 100> × Increment>

Increment
Power increments are a mechanic that increases the Move Power by a certain multiplier according to either a Skill or the Sync Move of a 6EX Tech Sync Pair. In case the unit does not have any of these considerations, the Increment should be only considered as 1. The following table displays the multiplier to use accordingly the situation happens:

Grid
This is the sum of all the power ups (Green Tiles) of the Move that were selected in the Grid of the Sync Pair.

Example: The following grid of Pikachu increases Thunderbolt’s Power by 32.

Image 1. Pikachu Grid

ΣSkillPowerUps
This is read as “Sum of Skill Power Ups” and should be interpreted as the result of adding all the Skill Power Ups that are activated while using a Move. All Sync Pairs have Skills assigned to them in different ways:
Passive Skills. These are the default skills that are always active as soon as you get the unit. Some units that can change Forms might have different Passive Skills exclusive to each form.
Lucky Skill. These are unlocked by using Lucky Scrolls and given a Skill with a Lucky Cookie. The Power Up skills are found in the Crunchy Cookies (the pink ones).
Grid Skills. These are obtained by activating Sync Grid Tiles with Sync Orbs. They’re colored as Red or Yellow. The Red Tiles are Skills assigned exclusively to one move while the Yellow Tiles are new Passive Skills for the whole unit.

From all the possible skills, the ones that matter for the Move Power are the ones that contain the text “powers up” in the description. After identifying them, you can spot other two characteristics in them:
Move Target. The Skills specify the type of moves they affect by indicating in the description the following:
Powers up moves. These ones apply to Regular Moves. Example: Power Reserves 9: Powers up moves in a pinch.
Powers up sync moves. These ones only affect the Sync Move. Example: Solar Flare 5: Powers up sync moves when the weather is sunny.
Powers up attacks. These affect both Regular Moves, Sync Moves and MAX Moves. Example: Critical Strike 2: Powers up attacks if they become critical hits.
Power Up Value. Most of the Power Up Skills have a number next to their name, like Power Reserves 9 or Paralysis Synergy 5. The ones who don’t indicate a value, like Haymaker or Ramming Speed, have an implicit value that’s linked to a characteristic of the unit or are Unique Skill that’s made specifically for the unit, like Marnie’s Mode Swing.

Using the Pikachu Grid displayed in the Image 1, you can get the following ΣSkillPowerUps scenarios:

ΣSkillPowerUps = 0

ΣSkillPowerUps = 0.5 [Paralysis Synergy 5]

If Pikachu with less than 33% HP uses Thunderbolt against a paralyzed target:
ΣSkillPowerUps = 0.5 [Power Reserves 5] + 0.9 [Power Reserves 9] + 0.5 [Paralysis Synergy 5]
ΣSkillPowerUps = 1.9

Gauge Power Up Skills
Regular moves
Power Flux is a Valued Power Up Skill that takes in consideration how full the Move Gauge is charged when you select the Move. The Value is used in the formula by multiplying it by 0.01 per Gauge.
Power Up(Value,Gauge) = Value × Gauge × 0.01

Examples:
If the user has Power Flux 2 and selects Ember (Gauge Cost: 1) while the Gauge Bar is at 4, the Skill Power Up will be 0.08.
Power Up(2,4) = 2 × 4 × 0.01 = 0.08
If the user has Power Flux 3 and selects Blast Burn (Gauge Cost: 4) as soon as possible (Gauge Bar at 4), the Skill Power Up will be 0.12.
Power Up(3,4) = 3 × 4 × 0.01 = 0.12
If the user has Power Flux 5 and selects Liquidation (Gauge Cost: 3) while the Gauge Bar is full, the Skill Power Up will be 0.3.
Power Up(5,6) = 5 × 6 × 0.01 = 0.3

Sync moves

Sync Power Flux is an Unvalued Skill Power Up that scales by 0.167 for each gauge present while selecting the Sync Move, the result is then rounded to the second decimal. In formula, it behaves like this:
Power Up(Gauge) = 2|Gauge × 0.167|

Single Stat Power Up Skills
These skills do not specify a Value in its Skill Name and their description goes like this:
The more the [user/opponent]’s [Stat] has been [raised/lowered], the more it powers up (sync) moves.

As it is stated, these Skills use a single Stat, excluding HP, as reference for the Power Up, and it is either by how many positive counts (times raised) or negative counts (times lowered) it has. This means that the Skill Power Up can have up to 6 different values different than 0. Also, another consideration is which type of Move the Skill affects, since the maximum Skill Power Up varies for Regular Moves (0.30) and Sync Moves (1.00). This Maximum Value is then divided by 6 and rounded to three decimal points to get the Stepping increase to be used by multiplying it by the count of the stat variation, being 0.05 for Regular Moves and 0.167 for Sync Moves.
Power Up(Count,Step) = 2|Count × Step|

Dual Stat Power Up Skills
These skills do not specify a Value in its Skill Name and are similar to Single Stat Power Up Skills. Their description goes like this::
The more the [user/target]’s [Stat1] and [Stat2] are [raised/lowered], the more it powers up (sync) moves.

The power increase functions as two Single Stat Power Up Skills, meaning that they are just two skills in one.
Multiple Stats Power Up Skills
These skills do not specify a Value in its Skill Name and their description goes like this:
The more the [user/target]’s stats have been [raised/lowered], the more it powers up (sync) moves.

This kind of Skill takes into consideration all the modifications in all the Stats, excluding HP, of either the user or opponent, by counting how many times their stats have been raised or lowered. Same as Single Stat Power Up Skills, these stats follow a Step power up but has different roundings and limitations:
Regular Moves Skill Step is 0.026 and the result is rounded down to the second decimal point. The maximum value it will reach is 1.1, meaning it takes 43 counts to reach its full potential.
Power Up(Count) = MIN(2<Count × 0.026>,1.1)
Sync Move Skill Step is 0.0667 and the result is rounded to the second decimal point. The maximum value it will reach is 1.2, meaning it takes 18 counts to reach its full potential.
Power Up(Count) = MIN(2|Count × 0.0667|,1.2)
A special note to this would be that if the Skill takes into consideration only the positive counts (raises), the opposite counts won’t be subtracted. Example: Power Loving counts the target’s lowered stats, if the enemy has -6 Attack, -4 Speed and +6 Sp. Attack, the count used is 10.

Part 2. Stat Ratio
This part refers to the ratio between the User’s Attack versus the Target’s Defense. By this aspect, the number obtained in this part is not rounded in its final result. The formula goes like this:
Stat Ratio = Attacker Stat × 0.5 / Defender Stat

Both Attacker and Defender Stat have the same formula while in calculation:
Stat = 0<(Form Stat + Grid + Theme) × Variation>

The 0.5 multiplication is an adjustment for the enemy stats. It has been found in tests that, without this multiplier, the enemy defenses always match with even numbers while their attack can match with a value that ends with either 0 or 0.5, as if their defense is always doubled while its attack is always halved during the calculations. Knowing this, the defenses described  in the scenarios at the start of the document would be like this:
Wallace’s target has 77 Sp. Defense.
Ethan’s target has 78 Sp. Defense.

Form Stat
The Form Stat corresponds to the value displayed in the Sync Pair screen. This won’t display any addition selected from the Sync Grid, but will include any Potential increases and, in case of alternate forms, a multiplier applied to the Base Form Stat (like Mega Evolutions or the Stance Change of Aegislash). The Form Stat can either be the Base Stat or a Modified Stat. In case of the latter, there are certain considerations to be taken:
If the Modified Stat has a Multiplier with decimals, the result is rounded up and then gets subtracted 1:
Modified Stat = 0〈Base Stat × Multiplier〉 - 1
If the Modified Stat is an integer Multiplier, it is just a simple multiplication:
Modified Stat = Base Stat × Multiplier

The considerations to determine what’s the Base Stat are only the amount displayed in the Sync Pair Stats Screen and the equipped Gear to the corresponding stat. Meaning that it would be calculated like this:
Base Stat = View Stat + Gear

The value displayed already takes into account any Potential increase that was applied to the unit, but in case you want to know how it works, it can be described like this: When a Sync Pair has its potential raised up to 20 times, it will gain a new Star, making it possible to do this repeatedly until the unit reaches 5 Stars with potential of 20 and opening the possibility for it to unlock the 6EX. The amount of stats gained per potential is different depending on the original star count of the Sync Pair and the stat that’s being raised:
If the Sync Pair’s original Star Count is less than 5, the HP is increased by 2 while the other stats are raised by 1. A full Increase would be 40 for HP and 20 for the rest stats.
If the Sync Pair’s original Star Count is 5, the HP is increased by 5 while the other stats are raised by 2. A full Increase would be 100 for HP and 40 for the rest stats.
Leaving us the following applicable formula:
Stat = Base + Increase × (Final Stars - Start Stars) + Potential × Step

Battle Conditions = 1.50 [Critical hit] × 2 [Supereffective] × 1.50 [Terrain boost]

Part 4. Rolls
Same as the mainline games, all the damage calculated have a possibility of doing up to 11 different values by simply multiplying the whole previous parts by the following numbers:

Damage Rolls = [0.90,0.91,0.92,0.93,0.94,0.95,0.96,0.97,0.98,0.99,1.00]
